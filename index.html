Choose an English skill

If you know where your weak points are, you can use one of the sections below to do more intensive practise with that particular skill.

Reading

Reading skill means quickly understanding words and sentences in texts and understanding their meanings to improve your language use. It helps you learn new words and see how they're used in different situations.

Practise Reading →

Writing

Writing skill is about putting your thoughts into words clearly and correctly, which helps you communicate in writing and improves your language. It's essential for emails, essays, and messages.

Practise Writing →

Listening

Listening skill means being able to understand what others are saying in conversations or recordings, which helps you follow discussions and enjoy movies or songs in English.

Practise Listening →

Speaking

Speaking skill involves expressing yourself fluently and accurately in conversations, helping you interact with others and share your ideas effectively.

Practise Speaking →

Vocabulary

Vocabulary skill involves learning and using new words and phrases to express yourself more clearly and accurately, which helps you understand and be understood in English conversations and texts.

Practise Vocabulary →

Grammar

Grammar skill involves understanding and using the rules of English to form sentences correctly and clearly, which helps you communicate effectively and avoid misunderstandings.
